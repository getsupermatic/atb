# Part 1 — Messaging foundation

## 1.1 The name and the idea

**ATB.** — the brand is **ATB**, carried with a full stop at the end. The full stop is part of the mark (as in the "atb-period" logo): a small, deliberate punctuation of confidence — *a statement, finished.* Use **ATB.** as the primary name and lead with it wherever possible.

*Where does the "Beyond" come from?* ATB stands for **At The Beyond**. *Beyond* means the farther side of something — outside the limits, the reach, or the understanding of what exists today. That's where frontier AI lives, and where the customer frontline has been left waiting. ATB. exists on that farther side: building the things that don't exist yet, for the people technology usually forgets.

Usage rules:
- **ATB.** (with full stop) as the primary brand mark and default name throughout.
- **At The Beyond** available for first mention, formal contexts, or where the full name adds warmth — but ATB. is the default.
- Never "AtBeyond," "At Beyond," or "the Beyond" in body copy.
- **Never use "Beyond" on its own** as the company's name or a shorthand for us. Other companies use the name "Beyond"; we protect ours by always being **ATB.** / **At The Beyond**. The *concept* of "the beyond" (the farther side, outside the limits) can be evoked in copy — but not as our name, and ideally not as the opening word of a sentence about us.
- Legal entity remains **At Beyond Limited** for footer/legal only.

> **Design/verbal note:** the "Beyond" idea is what makes the retro-computational editorial imagery feel earned — horizons, orbits, the farther side of a curve. Copy should reach for that register (edges, frontiers, the far side, what's next) without overusing the word "beyond" itself.

## 1.11 Tone of voice

**Confident, plain, and built — not hyped.** ATB has shipped a live product and doesn't need to shout. Where competitors pick a fight ("Everyone sells AI efficiencies. We build for growth."), ATB's edge is quieter and more specific: *we build for the frontline, and it's live.*

Five rules:
1. **Show the shipped thing.** Prefer proof to adjectives. "Live with Burger King UK" beats "revolutionary."
2. **Short sentences carry weight.** Vary rhythm; land on a short line.
3. **Name the frontline concretely** — store floor, drive-through, field, contact centre — not "the enterprise."
4. **Editorial, not corporate.** Think a good long-read, not a pitch deck. Reach for the "Beyond" register: edges, horizons, the far side, what's next.
5. **Never overclaim on AI.** Confidence without mysticism. No robots, no "sentience," no magic.
